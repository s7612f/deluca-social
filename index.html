Typography

The view? Bellissimo.
Cormorant Garamond Italic — all display text, captions, headlines. Always large. Never light weight at small sizes.
De Luca · cucina & bar
DM Sans Light — logo text, labels, addresses. Spaced out (letter-spacing 0.05em).

Voice — do

  • Italian phrases: Bellissimo, Stasera, Prenota, Fatto a mano
  • Rhetorical questions: "Fancy a bite?"
  • Short lines — let the photo lead
  • Warm and direct, like a charming host
  • Reference Cambridge specifically
  • Celebrate occasions: graduation, date night

Voice — don't

  • Banned words: authentic, passion, love, amore, amazing, delicious, journey, nestled
  • Emoji overload or exclamation marks
  • Generic hashtags (#foodie #yummy #instagood)
  • Corporate or formal tone
  • Captions over 200 characters for feed posts
  • Crimson as "wine red" — it's contemporary, not traditional

Strategy
When to post.
What to post.
A simple weekly rhythm — two stories and one feed post. Batched on Sunday in 30 minutes, scheduled to go out at the right times.
Weekly posting schedule
Monday
9–11am
Story 1 — mood / atmosphere. One beautiful image. No CTA. Let the photo do the work.
Wednesday
9–11am
Story 2 — offer or event. Booking prompt, Piano Bar reminder, seasonal special. Always add a link sticker.
Friday
5–7pm
Feed post — hero content. Best image of the week. 150–200 char caption. 4–5 hashtags.

How to use a template

Three steps

1.
Download the SVG
Click ↓ SVG on any template. The file saves to your downloads folder.
2.
Open in Canva
In Canva, create a new design at the right size. Uploads → Upload files → select the SVG. Drag it onto the canvas as a background layer.
3.
Add your photo & post
Drop your photo into the grey placeholder zone. Copy a caption from the Captions tab. Export and post.
